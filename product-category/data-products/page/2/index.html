MBON Portal Overview

May 2016. The beta version of the Marine Biodiversity Observation Network portal and data catalog was launched with more than 100 historical datasets from Florida, California and the Arctic, provided by multiple agencies and programs, including the Long-Term Ecological Research (LTER) programs, fisheries (e.g. NOAA’s Fisheries Science Centers), seabird and marine wildlife institutions and others.

Explore the MBON Data Catalog

The MBON Portal was developed by Axiom Data Sciences in collaboration with the MBON DMAC teams. The portal enables researchers and resource managers to sort, or filter specific species data by time, taxon, area, habitat (benthic class), etc., and recompute and graph the results through an intuitive online interface.  This enables researchers to assess changes over time, for given species and location.  Researchers can select and layer multiple datasets for a region — to assess connections between habitat change and species change.

MBON is the first portal to assess biodiversity by using specific indices. Click here to map data from the Florida Keys Reef Fish Visual Census and sort species by different biodiversity indices.  

Next steps: Adding Environmental Data The MBON team is in the process of adding data from water quality monitoring programs (e.g. dissolved oxygen, nutrients, chlorophyll-a, salinity). For Florida, environmental data from 31 field surveys (2006-2016) conducted by the University of Miami’s R/V Walton Smith as part of the South Florida program (SFP), and historical and near real-time oceanographic and marine meteorological data from NOAA’s National Data Buoy Center, water level data from NOAA’s National Ocean Survey, and salinity and temperature data from the Everglades National Park will be added to the ERDDAP server, for enrollment into the MBON Data Catalog.

The MBON PIs and NOAA program managers have been meeting with key users and experts to collect input and discuss specific research and monitoring uses.  The outcomes of these meetings are helping to refine potential technical, visual and functional changes to support user needs, and develop tutorials and instructions.

FWRI Enhances EcoSim model

Oct 2016. Luke McEachron at the Florida Fish and Wildlife Conservation Commission (FWC) is currently conducting QA/QC tests to a time dynamic ecosystem model for the Florida Keys National Marine Sanctuary (FKNMS). The initial results indicate the model has successfully quantified changes in trophic relationships and diet shifts throughout the Florida Keys from 1994 and 2012.

Additionally, the team estimated several ecosystem indicator statistics that describe the state of the Florida Keys ecosystem in terms of trophic dynamics over the same period. We are currently working through the literature to determine which indicators are redundant, and which are the most ecologically meaningful in terms of supporting Sanctuary Condition Reports.

FWC has also been instrumental in predicting coral out planting locations throughout the Florida Keys. We successfully conducted field work this fall to validate outplanting predictions and found very good agreement between in situ observations and model predictions. This work will be used to support future outplanting strategies, in addition to elucidating the environmental conditions that encourage coral outplanting success and failure. FWC will demonstrate an interactive version of the coral outplanting model at a NOAA workshop to Advance the Science and Practice of Caribbean Coral Restoration, Nov. 15-17, 2016, Fort Lauderdale, FL.

The team is writing an article “Prioritizing suitable restoration sites of the threatened Acropora cervicornis along the Florida Reef Tract using a species distribution modeling approach.”
